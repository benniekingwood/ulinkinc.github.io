Problem & Approach

A $1.5 billion budgeted government agency that provided multiple services to our country through defense, Counterdrug prevention, border and air patrol. These services, in particular Counterdrug services, were supported by fragmented web applications to its soldiers in field and base operations.There were major concerns with the technology of the web applications the soldiers utilized for their operations. These concerns were centered around the age of technology the applications were built upon and their ability to support the growing mission data, along with the validations of the data input by the field soldiers. Data integrity became a major concern that caused inaccuracies in the end of year reporting. This also affected determinations of proper budgeting for the Counterdrug program at the end of each year. As the amount of data input grew with the increase of field missions, the ability for the existing web applications to scale were limited by the technologies the platforms were built upon. The goal of our work was to design and develop a single consolidated cloud application that could support the demands of the growing Counterdrug prevention program.

In 60 days, we:

  • Conducted a series of meetings to gather the necessary legacy codebase and analyzed the web applications for potential problem areas
  • Evaluated the necessity of each separate application
  • Met with owning government representatives to hear areas of concern and ideal improvements as reported by their field operatives
  • Determined additional requirements needed based on the number of field operatives utilizing the applications
  • Determined that multiple applications were unnecessary causing more points of failure and increasing the likelihood of human error during data input in the field

Solution & Result

A consolidated application with a consistent, easy to navigate interface that would reduce the number of clicks, thus reducing time of input keeping field operatives engaged in missions and spending less time navigating the application. One datastore with cached stored procedures and views that would optimize reports on data inputs. Strict data input validations to increase data integrity, allowing for more accurate end of year reports.

The Counterdrug program now has a fluid, scalable, single consolidated cloud application that performs efficiently and maintains data integrity. Field soldiers can now quickly input mission data and manage personnel, thus more accurately reporting back to superiors and getting a better sense of budget needs to continue missions each year. Personnel now need less training to utilize the application and can quickly duplicate their knowledge on application usage to incoming field soldiers.

Impacts

Improvements in the amount of time to data input, accuracy of data reported by the field, and quality of reports to government.

  • Improvements of the user interface resulted in less training required for the duplication of knowledge
  • More accurate reports lead to additional funding, keeping the program stable as it continued to grow
  • Prevention of billions in drug revenue each year
